Frequently Asked Questions

A tankless water heater heats water on demand instead of storing it. When you turn on the hot water tap, cold water travels through a pipe into the unit where an electric element or a gas burner heats it to the desired temperature.

Yes, tankless water heaters are more energy-efficient because they only heat water when you need it, reducing standby energy losses associated with maintaining a tank of hot water.

The size of the tankless water heater should be determined by the flow rate (measured in gallons per minute) and the temperature rise needed for your household. It’s best to consult with a professional to assess your specific needs based on the number of fixtures and their hot water demand.

Installation can be more complex than traditional water heaters, especially if upgrading your gas line, venting, or electrical system is needed. It's recommended to hire a licensed professional to ensure proper installation and safety.

Tankless water heaters can last more than 20 years with proper maintenance, which is longer than traditional tank water heaters that typically last 10-15 years. Regular descaling and system checks can help enhance longevity.
