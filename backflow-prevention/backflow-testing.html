Importance of Backflow Testing

Frequently Asked Questions

Backflow testing is a procedure to verify that a backflow preventer, a device that prevents contaminated water from flowing backward into the clean water supply, is functioning properly.

Backflow testing is important to ensure the safety of drinking water. It helps to prevent harmful contaminants from entering the potable water system due to changes in water pressure.

Typically, backflow testing should be conducted annually. However, local regulations and specific conditions at a property may require more frequent testing.

Backflow testing should be performed by a certified backflow tester or a licensed plumber with the necessary credentials and training to ensure the device is tested and maintained properly.

If a backflow device fails the test, it must be repaired or replaced to restore its function. The failure typically indicates an issue that could allow contaminants to enter the water supply, so prompt attention is needed.
