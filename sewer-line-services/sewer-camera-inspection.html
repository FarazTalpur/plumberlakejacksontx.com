Frequently Asked Questions

A sewer camera inspection is a process where a waterproof camera is inserted into the sewer lines to visually inspect the condition of the pipes, identify blockages, leaks, or other potential issues.

Sewer camera inspections help identify potential problems such as clogs, root intrusion, leaks, and deteriorating pipes. Early detection can prevent costly repairs and ensure the proper functioning of the sewer system.

It's recommended to have a sewer camera inspection done regularly, especially if you're buying a new home, experiencing frequent plumbing issues, or notice unpleasant odors. However, the frequency may vary depending on the age and condition of the sewer system.

Signs include recurring clogs, slow drains, frequent backups, unpleasant odors, unsanitary yard conditions, and old or outdated piping systems. Any of these issues may warrant a camera inspection to pinpoint the problem.

A typical sewer camera inspection can take anywhere from 30 minutes to a couple of hours, depending on the length and condition of the sewer lines and the complexity of the issues being inspected.
