Sub-Services of Trenchless Sewer Repair

Pipe Bursting

This method involves replacing old pipelines by bursting them while simultaneously installing new pipes. It's ideal for replacing main water and sewer lines efficiently.

Pipe Lining (Cured-In-Place Pipe)

This technique involves inserting a resin-saturated liner into the existing pipes, which is then cured to form a new pipe within the old one. It's perfect for repairing minor damages and root intrusion.

Directional Drilling

This allows for the installation or replacement of pipes and conduits with minimal surface disruption, ideal for crossing under roads or driveways.

Frequently Asked Questions

Trenchless sewer repair is a method of fixing or replacing underground pipes without the need for extensive digging, minimizing disruption to the surrounding area.

Trenchless repair typically involves using methods such as pipe lining or pipe bursting, where new pipes or linings are inserted into the existing pipeline, either through an access point or by splitting the old pipe apart.

Benefits include less disruption to landscaping, faster repair times, reduced mess compared to traditional methods, improved pipe durability, and potentially lower costs due to less labor and restoration work.

The time required for trenchless sewer repair varies depending on the complexity of the project, but it generally takes one to two days, significantly quicker than traditional sewer repair methods which can take several weeks.

The initial costs of trenchless methods can be higher than traditional repairs, but they often save money in the long run by reducing landscaping restoration costs, minimizing labor, and extending the life of the pipe.
